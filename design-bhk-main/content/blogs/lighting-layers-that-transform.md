Successful lighting combines ambient, task, and accent layers. The best lit homes feel inviting at dawn, energizing at midday, and intimate after sunset.

## The Three Layers

Think of lighting like a symphony—each layer plays a distinct role, but together they create harmony.

### Ambient Light

Start with a warm ambient base that fills the space. This is your foundation:

- **Recessed ceiling lights** with warm dimmable LEDs
- **Pendant fixtures** that cast soft pools of light
- **Wall sconces** that graze surfaces gently

Aim for 2700K-3000K color temperature—warm enough to flatter skin tones, bright enough for daily activities.

### Task Lighting

Add targeted task lighting for functionality. This is where precision matters:

1. **Under-cabinet strips** in kitchens
2. **Desk lamps** with adjustable arms
3. **Reading lights** beside beds and chairs
4. **Vanity lighting** at face height

> "Task lighting should be bright enough to work by, but never harsh."

### Accent Light

Finish with accent light to add drama and highlight textures. This is the jewelry of your lighting scheme:

- **Picture lights** that illuminate artwork
- **Uplights** that graze textured walls
- **Cabinet lighting** that showcases objects
- **Landscape lighting** that extends the home outward

## Dimmers Are Essential

Every light should be dimmable. The ability to adjust intensity transforms a space throughout the day:

| Time of Day | Ambient | Task | Accent |
|-------------|---------|------|--------|
| Morning | 80% | 100% | 40% |
| Afternoon | 60% | 100% | 60% |
| Evening | 30% | 50% | 80% |
| Night | 10% | 0% | 20% |

## Natural Light Integration

The best lighting schemes work *with* natural light, not against it. Consider:

- **Sheer window treatments** that diffuse harsh sunlight
- **Mirrors** that bounce light deeper into rooms
- **Light-colored walls** that reflect rather than absorb

Automated shades can adjust throughout the day, preserving views while controlling glare.

## Common Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls:

- **Over-lighting** — more fixtures don't mean better light
- **Single sources** — relying on one overhead fixture creates harsh shadows
- **Cool temperatures** — anything above 3500K feels clinical
- **Neglecting controls** — switches should be intuitive and accessible

## The Transformation

When layers work together, a home feels alive. Morning coffee by soft window light. Afternoon work sessions with focused task illumination. Evening conversations bathed in warm ambient glow.

This is lighting that doesn't just illuminate—it *elevates*.
