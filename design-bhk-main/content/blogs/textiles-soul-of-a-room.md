Imagine a room with four painted walls, wooden floors, and leather furniture. It feels stark. Now, add a hand-knotted wool rug, linen curtains pooling on the floor, and velvet cushions. The space is instantly transformed. Textiles are the soul of a room.

## Building a Textural Palette

When selecting textiles, contrast is key. If your sofa is a smooth, cool leather, pair it with chunky, knitted throws or tactile bouclé cushions. If your upholstery is fine linen, introduce a rich, heavy velvet for visual weight.

- **Linen:** Offers a relaxed, organic feel. Perfect for drapery as it filters light beautifully.
- **Velvet:** Adds immediate luxury and depth, absorbing light to create rich, saturated color.
- **Wool:** Introduces warmth and structure. A wool rug anchors a space better than almost anything else.

## The Pattern Play

Mixing patterns can be intimidating, but there is a straightforward formula for success:

1. **Start with a hero pattern:** This is usually the largest scale pattern and carries the main color palette. (e.g., a bold floral rug).
2. **Add a secondary pattern:** This should be roughly half the scale of your hero pattern. (e.g., a geometric cushion).
3. **Finish with a small-scale or textured solid:** This grounds the mix. (e.g., a subtle pinstripe or a textured solid knit).

## Drapery Rules

Never underestimate the power of curtains. To make a ceiling appear taller, hang drapery hardware as close to the ceiling as possible—not just right above the window frame. Let the fabric 'kiss' the floor or puddle slightly for a look of relaxed indulgence. Custom window treatments are an investment that instantly elevates the perceived value of your home.
