Color is perhaps the most powerful tool in interior design. It has the ability to transform the perceived temperature, size, and function of a room entirely. Understanding color psychology is crucial for creating environments that feel tailored to their purpose.

## Warm vs. Cool Tones

Warm colors like terracotta, ochre, and soft peach advance visually, making a large, cavernous room feel intimate and cozy. They are stimulating and highly social, making them excellent choices for dining rooms or living areas where conversation flows.

Cool tones, such as soft sage, pale blue, or stormy grey, recede. They expand a space visually and are intrinsically calming. These are ideal for bedrooms and bathrooms—places designed for rest and rejuvenation.

## The Power of Neutrals

Neutrals shouldn't be boring. A sophisticated palette of greige, taupe, or warm ivory provides a restful backdrop that allows the architecture, art, and people in the room to take center stage. When working with neutrals, the focus shifts to texture. Layering different tones and finishes prevents the space from falling flat.

## Testing Your Palette

Always test paint colors on the walls before committing. The same hue can look dramatically different depending on the natural lighting in your space and the time of day. Try large swatches on multiple walls and observe how they change from dawn until dusk.

Ultimately, your home's color palette should reflect your personal energy and the specific mood you want to cultivate in each room.
